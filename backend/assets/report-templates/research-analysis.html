Executive Summary

Energy Infra Partners (EIP) manages approximately 2 GW of onshore wind across 4 clusters in the Nordics. The site under evaluation — Project Odin — is part of the Mark Buctin cluster near Luleå in Sweden's SE1 bidding zone, with 2-3 GW of total wind capacity across multiple development phases.

Following Sweden's July 2023 energy tax increase on data centers (from 0.6 to 36 öre/kWh), EIP and Sangha are pivoting the opportunity assessment from pure Bitcoin mining toward AI compute co-location, with BTM mining as an interim monetization strategy during the development phase. This mirrors the industry-wide trend: every significant mining operator in Sweden (Hive Digital, Northern Data) is converting to AI/HPC.

SE1 and SE2 consistently trade at a steep discount to southern Sweden due to the SE2-SE3 transmission bottleneck (~8 GW physical corridor, still congested). Production surplus in the north cannot flow south fast enough, creating a structural price advantage for northern consumers.

Seasonal Dynamics

  • Spring (Apr-Jun): Lowest prices, frequently negative. Spring flood hydro forces generation at a loss. SE2 had 679 negative-price hours in 2025.
  • Summer: Low demand + moderate wind = continued low prices.
  • Winter: Higher demand (heating), reduced wind — prices rise but remain well below SE3/SE4.
  • Wind events: Extreme oversupply drives prices to zero or negative, especially in SE1/SE2.
BTM Implication: A direct PPA with EIP for Odin wind output could realistically achieve €15-30/MWh baseload, with significant hours at zero or negative. This is materially cheaper than the €49-59/MWh gross figure in the original SE2 report (which included grid costs and energy tax).

SE1 Price Trajectory Risk

SE1 is projected to flip from net exporter to net importer of electricity by 2026-2030. Massive new industrial demand is incoming:

  • H2 Green Steel (Stegra): 13-15 TWh/year at full capacity
  • HYBRIT/LKAB: ~5 TWh/year for fossil-free sponge iron
  • Combined projection: 46.1 TWh of new demand in SE1 by 2030

This could raise SE1 spot prices significantly, making BTM power more valuable (higher opportunity cost) but also making a fixed PPA more attractive relative to rising grid prices.

2. Energy Tax — The Deal-Maker or Breaker

The July 2023 change removed tax benefits for all data centers — not just crypto miners. Both Meta-style facilities and Bitcoin mines pay the same 36 öre/kWh. The Ministry of Finance argued the exemption didn't create expected jobs.

Enforcement Climate

  • 18 of 21 investigated data centers were found concealing crypto mining to claim tax benefits
  • Swedish tax authority demanded SEK 990M (~$90M) in back taxes from miners
  • As of Jan 2025, any mining operation >0.5 MW must publicly report real-time energy use
  • Boden caps new mining at 5 MW; Kiruna demands 90% renewable verification

BTM Tax Question

Priority DD Item: BTM Energy Tax Treatment

The energy tax applies to electricity consumed from the grid. The critical legal question: does electricity consumed behind the meter from a directly connected wind farm incur the same 36 öre/kWh tax?

If BTM power is treated as self-generation by the wind farm operator (not "supply" to a consumer), it may fall outside the tax regime entirely. This needs definitive guidance from Swedish tax counsel — it is the single biggest variable in the economic model.

Industry Response: Projekt Datorhall

A coalition of Swedish data center operators is pursuing five legal pathways against the tax increase, claiming €680M+ in losses. Includes EU Commission complaint, Energy Charter Treaty arbitration, and Francovich damages. No resolution expected before 2027-2028.

Why SE1 for AI Compute

  • Climate: PUE of 1.07 achievable (Meta Luleå). Free air cooling year-round saves 20-30% on total energy vs. warm climates.
  • Power cost: SE1 spot at €25-28/MWh (2024 avg) — among lowest in Europe.
  • Fiber: Nordic Wave project (GlobalConnect) — 27 Tbps Stockholm-Luleå, completion mid-2026.
  • Validation: Meta's 724 MW Luleå campus proves hyperscale viability.
  • Waste heat: Cold climate + long heating season = opportunity to sell waste heat to district heating networks.

Phased Strategy Recommendation

Phase 1 (0-18 months): BTM Bitcoin mining as initial monetization. Fast to deploy, flexible load profile, instant revenue. Mining acts as a "buyer of first resort" for wind output during development.

Phase 2 (12-36 months): AI compute infrastructure buildout. Requires Tier-III facilities, fiber connection, cooling infrastructure, and customer contracts. Higher margins, better regulatory treatment, more sustainable long-term economics.

Phase 3 (ongoing): Hybrid operation — mining absorbs excess/curtailed wind, AI compute runs on baseload. FCR-D participation from mining loads provides ancillary revenue.

Mining-as-Demand-Response

Hive/Sympower precedent: Proven >8 MW FCR-D delivery from Boden mining facility. Response: 5 seconds for 50% curtailment, 30 seconds for full load. Mining loads have a structural advantage over batteries — they can shed hundreds of MW instantly at GW-scale clusters.

At Project Odin's 2-3 GW scale, even modest FCR participation would be material. However, do not model at 2022-2023 prices — use €6-20/MW/h as the conservative baseline. The original SE2 report's €25-30/MW/h estimate for FCR-N was from the high-water mark.
